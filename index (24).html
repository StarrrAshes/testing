Verdex is a student-led citizen science project tracking microplastic pollution in Long Island's coastal waters. It operates a single founding team at Wellington C. Mepham High School and publishes to a public dataset at verdexdata.org.

The project began as NEDN, a proposed multi-school network. When the co-founder went inactive in early 2026, the project rebuilt from a network premise to a single-team execution premise — one site sampled repeatably under a published protocol, before any expansion.

Partner expansion opens after six consecutive protocol-compliant sessions and closure of at least one external methodology review.

The gap Verdex addresses is specific: documented microplastic contamination exists in Long Island's coastal waters, but no community-facing dataset shows what conditions look like at named sites near residents' own neighborhoods. Verdex publishes those numbers as they are collected, honestly disclosed with limitations, and open to anyone who wants to use them.

Methodology · VSS v1.1

Adapted from NOAA. Reviewed. Published.

The Verdex Standard Sample Protocol is adapted from NOAA's Marine Debris Monitoring and Assessment Project. Version 1.1 adds procedural blanks, airborne contamination controls, and field hygiene rules.

01 · Field

Collect

Ten 1-liter samples plus one fixed field blank and one rotating blank (field duplicate on odd sessions, transport on even). Source-water rinse, foil lid barrier, 5–10 cm depth, tide and prior-24hr rainfall logged.

VSS v1.1 §5

02 · Lab

Screen

Sieve at 100 µm, stereo microscope examination, hot-needle confirmation on ambiguous particles. Reagent blank before samples, sieve carry-over blank between samples, airborne blank across the session.

VSS v1.1 §5.3, §6

03 · Publish

Report

Blank event totals published with sample counts. Flagged samples remain in the dataset with contamination annotation, never removed. Limitations stated on every report.

VSS v1.1 §9

Read the protocol, versioned change log, and reviewer credits. VSS v1.1 was reviewed by Aiden Tran (Field Operations Lead) and is under external review by Dr. Luis Medina Faull (SoMAS, Stony Brook) and Cassidy Freudenberg (Town of Hempstead).
